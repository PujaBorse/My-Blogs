an essay on choosing

Dilemma

P Pooja Borse · Apr 1, 2026 · 3 min read

Since childhood, I have always found myself standing between two choices. No matter how much I try to filter things out, no matter how much I try to simplify, I somehow always end up in the middle — pausing, thinking, wondering… should I go to the right or to the left?

Over time, I built a method for myself. I sit down, take a deep breath, and start writing. The pros — the benefits of choosing each option. Then the cons — the ones I believe I can tolerate at my level best. It feels logical. It feels structured. And sometimes… it even works.

But life is not always that simple.

Because if I go by everything I have read, heard, learned, experienced, or believed, then every chance we get, we are supposed to choose something that takes our breath away.

And yet, when I stand on the edge of that thought, I pause.

What if I can't breathe after that? (Haha… a funny thought, but a real one.)

Because my life, as it is right now, is beautiful. I want to live it fully — in whatever, wherever I am today. This moment, this phase, this version of life… it matters to me. I want to breathe in it, not rush past it.

And then comes the quiet question — What if I am not ready?

But honestly… who ever is?

No one is truly ready for anything. We weren't even ready when we stepped into this life. Things just happen. And somewhere along the way, I came across a thought that stayed with me —

"being ready is not a feeling, it's a decision."

And just like that, I find myself back at the same place again.

What decision should I make? Right or left? Or maybe… it was never just about choosing between two directions.

Maybe life isn't a straight road with clear signs. Maybe it's an open ground — where you can walk, pause, run, go zigzag, or even create a path where none exists. And maybe that's what makes it so overwhelming… because you don't always know what lies ahead.

Sometimes, we believe we are the ones making decisions. But lately, I feel something different. Maybe life makes them for us. Or maybe… life chooses us for certain decisions.

And what we call "trying our best" is simply this — showing up, giving everything we have, moving forward without knowing the outcome, and still choosing to believe in our effort.

That's how I deal with my dilemmas. I give it everything I can… and then I let it be.

Because deep down, I believe — if something is truly mine, it will find its way to me. And if it's not, no matter how tightly I hold on, it will eventually slip away.

It sounds simple when written. Almost poetic. But in reality… it's not always that easy. Overthinking makes sure of that.

Still, maybe with time, we learn. We learn to sit with uncertainty. We learn patience. We learn that not every ball is meant for us to play. And if it comes into our court, we give it our best shot. If it doesn't… maybe it was never ours to begin with.

I've heard this many times before. But now… I think I am finally beginning to understand it.

That's how I deal with my dilemmas. What about you? — Pooja
