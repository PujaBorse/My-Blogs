an essay on habits

You Are the Output of Your Habits.

P Pooja Borse · Aug 3, 2022 · 2 min read

When I was in school, as per the school schedule, we have so many things which are mandatory on the daily to-do list — for example, morning prayers, clean uniforms, meditation, etc. At that time, if I encounter someone senior [outside of school] who is not following the same, I look at them as someone who is not good enough. Now I am that someone, LOL!

Nowadays I started feeling that you are nothing but the output of your habits. I have heard this sentence many times, so it is not new to me, but of course, it is new to my future version, which will be there in the next few years.

I have seen my grandmother has the habit of revising things — which thing was placed at which location, who said what, and what is the authentic process of preparing any specific dish, etc. This habit makes her remember her daily tablets, how many tablets at a time, what is the colour of the tablet, and the size as well. So she is not dependent on us for the same.

My siblings were playing computer games from the time we were in school. Now everyone is doing their best and working as professionals but never miss the time to play together the new advanced computer games. The habit of exploring new games and developing skills in them just by playing helped them a lot in many ways, and it is another stress buster as well.

One of my relatives has a habit of travelling. Whenever we travel, he never sleeps and keeps checking on where we are now and how much distance is left. He adopts this as a habit, and now he knows more than half of India by road only. There are many more such stories around me.

"the behaviour of a person is nothing but the output of his habits."

When I observe these things in my close people, I believe the behaviour of the person is nothing but the output of his habits. If we adopt the right habit at the right time, it will help us in the future.

choosing the habit, one small day at a time. — Pooja
