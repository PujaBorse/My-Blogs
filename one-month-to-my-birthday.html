a note to a younger self

One Month to My Birthday

P Pooja Borse · Sep 4, 2023 · 2 min read

Picture this: it's a lazy Saturday night, we're beat from the week, and we decide it's time to treat ourselves to our all-time favorite food. Treating each other is our thing, the best part of our relationship. So, I'm on the phone with my sister, venting about how much I can't stand my coworkers at the office lately. It's been seriously bothering me. Her response is brutally honest —

"remember, your job brought you to Bengaluru."

It hits home. After our chat, we order our favorite food, grab a pastry for dessert, watch a movie, and go to bed.

Now, I don't know why, but I wake up at a crazy early 4 AM. It feels almost illegal to be up this early, especially on a Sunday. It's rare, but it happens. I grab my phone, start scrolling through Instagram, and stumble upon a post by a sports celeb who's turning 28. It hits me — I'm turning 28 in just a month. And here I am, comparing myself to these celebs who seem to have it all.

You see, when people look at your life, they often sum it up based on your job, income, and lifestyle. The more you achieve when you're young, the more people applaud you. I used to have those same aspirations in my 20s. But now, I'm starting to see the other side of the story.

What if you're 27 or 28 and still searching for your dream job? What if you're learning and growing every day, trying to become a better person? What if you're the rock for your family, bridging the generation gap instead of running away from it? What if you're focused on your health, healing from past hurts, and handling life's tough moments? What if you're grinding to keep your business afloat in a changing market?

What about that side of the story? It's not about overachieving; it's about soaking up knowledge and experience. Maybe this is the path to something amazing, something big. Perhaps the most exciting part of your story isn't in your late twenties, but it's just around the corner, maybe in your thirties or beyond. Some stories have a way of surprising us when we least expect it.

So, here's to embracing the journey, no matter where it leads. The best chapters may still be unwritten. Cheers to life's twists and turns that make it one heck of an adventure!

here's to the pages not yet written. — Pooja
