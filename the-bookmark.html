an essay on bonding slowly

The Bookmark

P Pooja Borse · Jun 16, 2022 · 3 min read

Do you like to read people? Are you curious to know the little habits of people which help them to grow? Do you find it difficult to interact with strangers? Have you witnessed the journey of people from strangers to close ones?

The answer to the above questions is probably yes in most cases, and that is the start of a friendship. Have you been in a situation where you need to interact with new members when you are not at all ready for that? I have been in such situations many times, and I often fail to build any bonding.

I feel when you keep growing you need to develop three habits for your betterment.

Give time

If you are looking for a deep connection, you need the patience to let it happen. Either you win or lose.

Move on

In case you fail at the above point, just don't waste more time and move on. Moving on will level you up.

Be kind

It doesn't matter who is next to you — always be kind.

Recently I was in a situation where I want to make a bond with a completely different person than me, but instead of failing this time, I decided to give time and decided to go with the flow.

Initially, it feels formal, with little talks, fake smiles, and avoiding the exact timing at the same place when you are staying together. After some days, the "it feels formal" becomes "it feels a little familiar." The uncomfortable talks become a little comfortable, and fake smiles become genuine. I had scheduled one activity daily where we two were together only so we can discuss any damn things.

Like this, slowly-slowly, I was reading the new story — the only difference is, it is not from the book, it is in front of me, playing, laughing, talking with me, and to my surprise, I was loving it. This is from my end only; how can I be sure what is there on the other side? After all, I don't want to fail this time, so I keep going with the flow.

One weekend, when we were chilling and talking about random things, she said, "I forgot to ask you in the morning, you love books, right?" I nodded with a smile. "So come, I want to show you something," she said. We both went to her wardrobe; she took her diary out and opened it, showed me the flowers she kept in the diary for years, and asked me to select one. All those flowers are paper flowers now, but still without missing any petals, and are beautiful.

I was confused about which to choose, and while choosing I asked her, "but these flowers, now for what?" She said —

"use it as your bookmark."

— with a smiley face. As far as I know, she doesn't like to share her things with anyone, but after this, I think I know the other side as well :D

pressed flowers make the best bookmarks. — Pooja
