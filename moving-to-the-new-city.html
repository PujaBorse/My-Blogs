an essay on starting over

Moving to the New City

P Pooja Borse · Aug 14, 2023 · 2 min read

Moving to the new city will not go out of style. Sometimes you move to the new city for your studies, sometimes for your job, sometimes with your family, sometimes with your partner after a wedding, and in many more scenarios. You start over again, and it never fails to give you new experiences which make you strong, happy, responsible, and an achiever.

I have a similar story about moving to the new city where I was going for the first time. I've never been to that city on any trip or for any other purpose. The first impression you will have is of the city's weather. If you enjoyed the city 90% because of the weather, you can manage the final 10% with ease.

The second step is to look for a house, specially without a broker as your pocket doesn't allow that extra brokerage. To be completely honest, it is not an easy process. You need to invest your weekends searching for the house as per your criteria. Even if you are lucky, it takes more than 4–5 weekends, otherwise you start second-guessing your choice to relocate.

Assume you are fortunate and have found a house or flat that meets your specifications. Then comes the exciting part of decorating the house. Yes, this is my favourite part. As we moved into our new home, we began to add necessities one by one. We are quite particular about the items we buy for the house, as they must be both decorative and functional. We didn't want to add anything that would make our home appear small or crowded.

Our hunt began with curtains. We were looking for curtains that were light in colour so that they would add a nice look to our living room, but when we closed them, they should block out the most light and make the room dark; also, the curtain cloth material should be considered. Aside from the above points, height is also an important consideration. We looked for the most frequent height, and it appears that the entrance curtains are most likely 7 feet in height. We also checked it for our door, and it fits well. Finally, we found our favourites — lavender colour curtains, and they are just so amazing for the living room.

"we didn't want to add anything that would make our home appear small or crowded."

Next, we were looking for something to beautify the area while also adding an antique aspect to our living room. Corner lamps immediately spring to mind when we think of lighting. Therefore, we were looking for a light that has an antique appearance and is reasonably priced. We found a charming antique lamp that was ideal for the corner. It increases the appearance of our living room.

This is how we enjoy our evenings at home.

Happy living! — Pooja
