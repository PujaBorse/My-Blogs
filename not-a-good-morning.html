Not a Good Morning!

P Pooja Borse · Sep 26, 2022 · 3 min read

The only thing left for me to do is to run, therefore I was running as fast as I could. I just want to keep going. I simply turned my head backward to gauge my distance from the monster, but there was no one, and the area was foggy. I turned to look in front, but there was still a lot of fog, and I was unable to see anything. I wanted to stop, but I had already started moving, so I went on. Suddenly, I slipped off the cliff, and I opened my eyes.

It takes me five minutes to realize that it is morning and I am still in bed. I'm already drained. I wake up to the alarm clock ringing.

I got out of bed and drank some water. I take a seat on the bed for a while. My heart is still beating fast despite my inability to comprehend why. This feeling is weird, not a good vibe. If I want to explain that feeling, then it would be as follows —

"it feels like I am missing something, it is there in front of me, but I am not able to identify what it is. If I ignore it now, it will be missed forever."

It is a scary feeling because I feel as though I will lose everything if I can't figure it out. I'm not sure where it came from, but this emotion has happened to me before; every time after that, a new big change happens in my life. It may be a coincidence, but maybe not if I link up some points.

I tried closing my eyes, taking a deep breath, and staying still for a while, but it didn't work. I left the room to find that everyone had already gotten up and started working. I stay out in the hallway. I wash my face, which helps me relax because the water was chilly. Now I feel better. I went back to my room and used my old method of writing thoughts down. No matter how positive or negative the thoughts are, I just jot them down in the notebook, rip the paper with my hands, and dump it in the trash thereafter. Peace, simply. It is 8:00 am when I looked at the clock.

I know what I wanted to do, so I leave the room and hug mummy and tell papa I'm going to my friend's house to grab the essential notes and will be back shortly. I choose to take the lengthy highway drive because the weather is pleasant. I keep moving fast enough to feel the sweetness of the cold wind on my hair. I say the phrase "Hakuna Matata" out loud anytime a notion enters my head, and then I carry on with what is going on. My heartbeats are normal, my mind is at peace, and there is no longer any unrest in my body after I took the U-turn and was on my way home.

I don't know about other things, but knowing what to do when my head is not in the right place has been the best thing I have done. I recalled my reason for leaving the house when Mommy asked me, "Oh, you bring your notes?" I retorted, "No Mommy, she said I'll bring it to you when done."

I believe having some drama is acceptable if you know how to control your emotions rather than whining. 😜

hakuna matata, always. — Pooja
