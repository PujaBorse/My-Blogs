an essay on healing

More Like a Machine

P Pooja Borse · Jul 26, 2024 · 2 min read

Sometimes it feels like life is easy, but then a second thought comes: at what cost? If you remove emotions from any situation, that situation feels like a mechanical connection or work. Emotions are the only tadka of life that keeps you going from crying to laughing. Most of the time, you try to keep your respect and ego aside and move with a deeper pure connection, but then someone close to you hurts that pure emotion so badly that you take time to move on from it. Sometimes it takes time, or sometimes you don't move on.

That emotion where your heart fails to move on keeps killing your same emotions in other scenarios as well, and you don't understand why you are behaving differently than what you were. The new you is still fragile to manage such a situation, where no matter how you try, you experience the same emotion again, and this time it is so deep that your heart retires from responding to it. But you still manage to keep going and doing the things you like —

"more like a machine and less like a person with emotion."

You heal in some time, and you manage to handle situations far better than before. Because now you are an adult, and you have only yourself to manage your emotions. Yes, of course, your close ones will be there, but as always, you have to fight with yourself to win. You won, and you are far better than before. You start to try as much as possible from your end to bloom again, to go and meet your core relations, to be happy from the heart, and you act far better than before.

Now, when everything is going well, you unknowingly enter into someone else's reason for the emotional damage cycle, and you blame yourself for it. Now your heart is too tired to even tell you what has happened exactly and what you are overthinking, and you keep going like a machine to achieve the things that cannot talk to you because now your heart wants the bare minimum, even if it is making you bad in someone else's life.

Because you have tried your best from your end from the time when you were so little, when you didn't know what adulting is, when your heart was so pure, and when you were so innocent to live. Being that as you grow old, you carry guilt, but you also carry satisfaction with you that you saved yourself, and you balance the gap.

That is how you again keep going — more like a machine, less like a person with emotion.

Emotions, work, feelings — still learning to hold all three. — Pooja
