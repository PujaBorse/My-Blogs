an essay on overthinking

If It Is Stuck in Your Head…

P Pooja Borse · May 31, 2022 · 3 min read

I googled the term "overthinking," and it shows the below result: overthinking is when you dwell or worry about the same thought repeatedly. People who overthink can be paralyzed by their worries and may struggle to make decisions or take action. Overthinking can be caused by — and can contribute to — depression, anxiety, and other mental health disorders.

I understand one thing from this: we should not google such terms, ha-ha! Isn't it extreme? Anything extreme is scary, so does "overthinking." I have seen many memes on overthinking, and it gets so hyped that nowadays it sounds like any skill to add, kidding!

There is little difference between overthinking and planning things. I would like to share this with the below experience.

Once I was on the bus, and two old people who seemed like friends were sitting behind my seat, discussing loudly — it is very easy to hear them. Their discussion suddenly turned from politics and finance to the young generation. The discussion went like this:

"nowadays, these young people use earphones and keep listening to songs and watching movies wherever possible. Just like at our time, we plan out the things for the day on the way to the office."

After listening to this, I did not agree with all points about the discussion, but the time management point they mentioned is pretty accurate. This may be called planning things out and not overthinking.

I have noticed when we are less sure about things, we overthink, but we can't go in the loop of overthinking again and again. When you know it, you know it. It is the conversation between you and you only. No one can interfere in between. No matter if it is just a random red dress you liked online or a big decision to move on. If it is stuck in your head, then just go for it with a plan.

Change is the peace and pain at the same time; the only difference is sometimes you find peace over pain or pain over peace — it is the output of the time you invest in it. I have read many times, "shoot for the moon, even if you miss you will land among stars," but in the worst case, if you miss stars too then? Is it okay? How can one decide that?

In all these, your funny brain will decide that we miss the stars here because we have the whole new moon waiting in another universe, and you will laugh at that thought. But maybe this is something your heart knows, and your brain fails to understand.

brain and heart, still arguing it out. — Pooja
