an essay on friendship · part one

Not Right Is Not Wrong!

P Pooja Borse · Apr 28, 2023 · 3 min read

I was standing alone, waiting for Kushagra to arrive, but he was late as usual. The restaurant was set to close in an hour, but there was no sign of Kushagra. I said, "Oh, at last, he's here." "I'm sorry, but my auto-wala can't find a shortcut due to the traffic," Kushagra stated. And so our dinner date continues. After two years, we were finally able to manage it.

We placed our order with two starters and two main course items, and the waiter moved our order to the kitchen. We have so much to say and so much to share from the past two years that an hour is not enough time. But it looks like we'll make it. His eyes twinkled as he talked about how he landed the job he had been preparing for months. He continues with his office co-workers, the technology they use, the projects he will be working on in the future using that technology, and how he will manage it all. Listening to him is a pleasure because I am glad for him.

And once he finished with his updates, I began with mine, detailing how my new job is going and where I am networking, what raises I received in this new job and how it is plenty for me for the time being, and how I planned to spend the extra money on myself. We were eating, cracking jokes, taking photos, and splitting bills, which has been our ritual since our first meeting. It doesn't matter how few or many bills there are, we will split them in two, and each of us must pay.

It was fun after so many days, and I didn't want to go home yet, so we decided to take the bus. We chose to walk because the bus station was close to the restaurant. We were walking when we began to have serious conversations. He apologized for not being able to attend my convocation and agreed to give me a present in exchange. I apologized because I was hyper at the moment and said a lot of things I shouldn't have said. "But don't you get angry with the people you care about?" I said. He pats my back and says, as if I care! We got to the bus stop and found some benches to sit on.

It was so peaceful and quiet, the roads were vacant, and the winds were gentle. I break the silence after a few minutes and say, "Do you think I was wrong in Shubham's case?" He was observing the vacant highways, and after hearing this query, he smiled a little and turned his face to me, putting his hands on my shoulder and saying, no it wasn't you, relax! A tear fell from my eye.

"your heart knows your truth, and you are right on your side."

If you can't fight for the respect you deserve, you won't get it.
