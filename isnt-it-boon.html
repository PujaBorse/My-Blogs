a Sunday, a story

Isn't It Boon?

P Pooja Borse · May 25, 2022 · 2 min read

"They say, whatever happens, happens for a reason — either you will learn or earn." I don't think it is that easy to believe, especially when you are a teenager.

Today's morning is special for me because it is Sunday. I took sufficient sleep and woke up at nearly 8:00 AM. After freshening up, I started with my favourite task of watering all the lovely plants in the angan [veranda]. The weather is quite pretty, with slow winds and cloudy all over. The only thing which is lacking there was music. I go back to my room, look for my phone, and play my favourite playlist, and it starts over there.

It is 2022, five years since our last year of Bachelor of Engineering. The only question that comes to my mind concerning five years is, "where do you want to see yourself in the next five years?" And I am sure no one has seen exactly what they are doing now after five years, not even me.

I remembered the video of my girls' group doing Babaji ka Thullu on the stage together in second year, and it was very hilarious. I think at that time memes were not there — when we say such things, it feels like, are we that old now, seriously? Haha! I will ask Ayushi to reshare it in the group. The fact is you can only feel the warmth of moments when they become memories.

Yesterday I received a text from Rashi saying isn't it amazing that Shravani is living her life to the fullest? And the only words I can reply with are, "yeah, finally!" Being in the group, we know how much she is craving the life she is living now.

Last week my best friend sent me a quote stating —

"just remember, five years ago, you dreamed about where you are now."

I understand completely what she wants to say. Another day, Purvi posted the story of celebrating her work anniversary, and I couldn't control it but replied with "your story after all such hard work can only be beautiful, shine girl," to which I received in return, "these words mean a lot to me, made my day!" That is the best part about friends — they know, you know, period. There are many more stories like this, and I love all of them.

Everyone has their own story in their unique way, and that story becomes the fuel to charge you every day. Places will keep changing, the surroundings will keep changing, and people will keep changing, but your story will always be with you from the start. The more you add to your story, the more you will enjoy. Now it is somewhat easier to understand than before — "they say, whatever happens, happens for a reason, either you will learn or earn."

still adding to the story. — Pooja
